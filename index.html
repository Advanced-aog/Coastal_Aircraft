Executive Summary

Strategic Acquisition

Advanced AOG & Avionics is acquiring Coastal Aircraft Maintenance (KSBA) and expanding the brand to our Georgia headquarters. The new partnership at our PDK facility secures 17 Part 91 managed aircraft, and capitalizes on $930,000 in expected corporate synergies.

Prime Location & Assets

We are anchoring operations across two 20,000 sq. ft. hangars located at PDK (Georgia) and SBA (California). The Coastal acquisition immediately brings in 5 maintenance vehicles and approximately $1M in high-value assets and equipment.

Multi-State Expansion

We are reactivating the Van Nuys outpost (projected to generate $50k/week with 4-6 technicians), bringing the Coastal brand to Georgia, and scaling our FL, NV, and CO markets to drive operational capital.

Purchase & Growth Strategy

Executing a highly favorable $1.5M acquisition of Coastal Aircraft Maintenance, while retaining the current owner on our board of directors for 12 months. We project continuous 30-40% growth between months 13 and 24, and plan to reopen the Teterboro (NJ) market in 2026, targeting $2M in new revenue.

The Opportunity

A Gap in the Market

PDK is a premier corporate aviation hub, yet demand for high-quality, responsive MRO services exceeds supply. Coastal Aircraft Maintenance is positioned to fill this gap with a service-first approach.

  • Instant Revenue: Inherits $500k-550k/year in hangarage revenue immediately upon purchase.
  • Hard Assets: Over $1M in combined appraised assets, including two separate 20,000 sq. ft. hangars (SBA and PDK) and 5 maintenance vehicles.
  • Fuel Farm Potential: The PDK location specifically features adjacent vacant property allowing for ramp expansion to maximize fuel sales.
  • Workforce Scale: Growing significantly by absorbing California teams and rapidly placing 4-6 technicians in Van Nuys.
