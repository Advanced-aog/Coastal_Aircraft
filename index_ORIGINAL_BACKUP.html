Executive Summary

Strategic Merger

Advanced AOG & Avionics is merging with Airmen MX to form Coastal Aircraft Maintenance. This consolidates 17 Part 91 managed aircraft and capitalizes on existing synergies.

Prime Location

Acquiring a 20,000 sq. ft. hangar at Peachtree DeKalb Airport (PDK), the second-busiest airport in Georgia, with direct taxiway access and expansion capabilities.

MRO Expansion

Transitioning from mobile services to a full-service "bricks and mortar" MRO. Capabilities include Phenom, Hawker, King Air, Citation, Falcon, and Learjet airframes.

Growth Trajectory

Projected revenue growth from $2.2MM (Current) to $8.7MM+ by 2028, driven by hangar acquisition, increased technician headcount, and fuel sales.

The Opportunity

A Gap in the Market

PDK is a premier corporate aviation hub, yet demand for high-quality, responsive MRO services exceeds supply. Coastal Aircraft Maintenance is positioned to fill this gap with a service-first approach.

  • Instant Revenue: Inherits $500k-550k/year in hangarage revenue immediately upon purchase.
  • Fuel Farm Potential: Adjacent vacant property allows for ramp expansion to maximize fuel sales.
  • Workforce Scale: Growing from 12 technicians at merger to 20 by 2027.

Financial Performance

Historical stability with aggressive post-acquisition growth.
